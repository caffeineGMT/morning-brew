⚡ Quick Takes

Today's biggest theme is unmistakable: the agent infrastructure stack is crystallizing fast. Microsoft's Copilot Cowork, Terminal Use's deployment platform, OpenAI's Promptfoo acquisition, and Dify's $30M raise all point to the same conclusion — the industry has moved past "can we build agents?" and is now laser-focused on "how do we deploy, secure, and govern them at scale?" The fact that OpenAI is buying a security startup specifically for agent red-teaming tells you everything about where the risk anxiety sits right now. Meanwhile, Anthropic's Code Review tool is a clever play: if AI is generating a flood of code, sell the tool that checks it. The fox guarding the henhouse, perhaps, but a useful fox.

The Anthropic-Pentagon saga continues to be the most consequential policy story in AI. A frontier lab suing the Department of Defense is unprecedented, and the outcome will shape how every AI company thinks about government contracts and use-case restrictions going forward. On the research side, Claude Opus 4.6 cracking its own benchmark encryption is both impressive and unsettling — a reminder that as these models get smarter, our evaluation methods need to evolve just as fast. And if BCG's "AI Brain Fry" study is any indication, the human side of the equation isn't keeping pace. We're building agents faster than we're learning to manage them.
