⚡ Quick Takes

The AI landscape is splintering in fascinating ways today. Cursor's Composer 2 is the clearest signal yet that the AI coding tools market is fragmenting beyond foundation model providers — specialized, vertically-tuned models built for narrow tasks can now rival the generalists at a fraction of the cost. Meanwhile, Google is aggressively building the commerce infrastructure for an agentic future: its expanded Universal Commerce Protocol gives AI agents real shopping capabilities (carts, catalogs, loyalty programs), while AI Studio's "vibe coding" feature turns natural language into multiplayer games. Google isn't just building AI — it's building the rails that AI agents will transact on.

On the research-to-product pipeline, Microsoft's superintelligence team shipping MAI-Image-2 as a consumer-facing product is a striking reminder that the gap between frontier research and shipped products is collapsing faster than anyone expected. And in funding news, Deeptune's $43M raise led by a16z to build simulated workplaces for AI training confirms that training data generation is becoming its own investable category — a meta-layer on top of the AI stack where the quality of synthetic environments directly determines the quality of the agents that emerge from them. The market for "AI training infrastructure" is no longer just GPUs; it's entire virtual worlds.
